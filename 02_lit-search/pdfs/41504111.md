# Inter-individual differentially methylated region-targeted EWAS reveals epigenetic signatures of early childhood adversity

**Reference:** Mayanagi, T., Yagi, J., Ohmomo, H., Akasaka, M., Fukumoto, K., Chiba, S., Komaki, S., Shimizu, A., Yanbe, T., Uchida, M., Yoshioka, Y., Ogawa, K., Ishikawa, C., Minabe, S., Ito, J., Ono, K., Kaneko, N., & Sobue, K. (2026). Inter-individual differentially methylated region-targeted EWAS reveals epigenetic signatures of early childhood adversity. *Epigenomics*, 18(1), 89-100. [PMID: 41504111]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** 44 Japanese children aged 6–12 years. The cohort was divided into an ACE group (n = 23, experiencing ≥4 types of adversity) and a control group (n = 21, zero adversity). All participants were psychotropic medication-naïve.
* **Sample Type:** Salivary genomic DNA.
* **EWAS Platform:** Inter-individual DMR-targeted capture-sequencing (CDMVv3 capture probe system) covering ~1.3 million CpG sites within regions of high inter-individual variability.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) occurring between birth and age five, assessed via 10-item structured interviews (e.g., abuse, neglect, household dysfunction).
* **Methodological Summary:**
* **Covariates:** Adjusted for age and sex using a linear regression model.
* **Significance Thresholds:** Suggestive threshold of p < 1.00 x 10^-5; Bonferroni-corrected threshold of p < 3.89 x 10^-8.

## Summary Notes

* **Functional Enrichment:** Genes near the identified DMRs were significantly enriched for biological processes including **signaling by GPCR**, **potassium channels**, and **negative regulation of peptide hormone secretion**. Disease-associated terms showed strongest enrichment for **neurodevelopmental disorders** and **Fragile X syndrome**.
* **EIF4G2 Significance:** The study identified a specific CpG cluster within the **EIF4G2** gene. EIF4G2 is involved in neuronal differentiation and synaptic plasticity; its dysregulation may link early stress to neurodevelopmental outcomes.
* **Cognitive Correlation:** Children in the ACE group had significantly lower Full-Scale IQ (FS-IQ) scores. Methylation levels at several loci (e.g., **OSBPL3** and **AQP1/MINDY4**) showed moderate negative correlations with FS-IQ.
* **Study Limitations:** The authors noted a relatively small sample size (n=44) due to strict inclusion criteria and the use of retrospective caregiver reports for ACE assessment, though the latter was mitigated by face-to-face professional interviews.
