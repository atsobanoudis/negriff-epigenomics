# Socioeconomic changes predict genome-wide DNA methylation in childhood

**Reference:** Liu, J., Cerutti, J., Lussier, A. A., Zhu, Y., Smith, B. J., Smith, A. D. A. C., & Dunn, E. C. (2023). Socioeconomic changes predict genome-wide DNA methylation in childhood. *Human Molecular Genetics*, 32(5), 709-719. [PMID: 35899434]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study analyzed 946 mother-child pairs from the Avon Longitudinal Study of Parents and Children (ALSPAC), a longitudinal birth cohort in the United Kingdom. The children were 49.9% female and predominantly White (97.1%).
* **Sample Type:** DNA methylation was measured from peripheral blood.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 BeadChip microarray.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Six socioeconomic position (SEP) indicators measured across three childhood periods (very early, ages 0-2; early, ages 3-5; and middle, ages 6-7). Indicators included job loss, income reduction, low family income, financial hardship, major financial problem, and neighborhood disadvantage.
* **Methodological Summary:** The researchers used a two-stage structured life-course modeling approach (SLCMA) to test three hypotheses: accumulation, sensitive period, and mobility. Covariates controlled for included child age at blood draw, race/ethnicity, sex, birthweight, maternal age, number of previous pregnancies, maternal smoking during pregnancy, and estimated cell type proportions. Significance was defined by an effect-size threshold of R2 > 3% and a multiple-testing correction of FDR < 0.05.

## Summary Notes

* **Dynamic SEP Impacts:** Changes in the socioeconomic environment (socioeconomic mobility and instability) were associated with greater differences in DNA methylation than stable exposure. Middle childhood was identified as a potentially sensitive period where socioeconomic disruptions are particularly impactful.
* **Enriched Pathways:** Top SEP-related CpGs were overrepresented in genes involved in neural development, immune function, metabolic processes, and membrane transport. Specifically, neighborhood disadvantage was linked to peroxisome-related genes (response to environmental pollutants), while financial hardship was linked to nutrient transport and metabolic pathways.
* **Tissue Correlation:** While DNA methylation was generally weakly correlated between blood and brain tissue, certain specific CpGs showed high correlation, suggesting peripheral blood can sometimes mirror epigenetic states in the brain.
* **Study Caveats:** The study was limited by its predominantly White population, which may restrict generalizability to other ethnicities. Additionally, while the SLCMA identified robust associations, only four CpGs passed the stringent FDR < 0.05 threshold, requiring further replication in larger, more diverse samples.
