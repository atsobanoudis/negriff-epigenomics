# The mediating role of KITLG DNA methylation in the association between childhood adversity and cortisol stress reactivity does not replicate in monocytes

**Reference:** Frach, L., Tierling, S., Schwaiger, M., Moser, D., Heinrichs, M., Hengstler, J. G., Walter, J., & Kumsta, R. (2020). The mediating role of KITLG DNA methylation in the association between childhood adversity and cortisol stress reactivity does not replicate in monocytes. *Psychoneuroendocrinology*, 116, 104653. [PMID: 32203862]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The sample consisted of 60 healthy adults (58 included in final analysis) aged 39 to 60 years. The group included a childhood adversity group (n = 30) matched with a control group (n = 30) for gender, age, and socioeconomic status.
* **Sample Type:** DNA was extracted from purified CD14+ monocytes.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Childhood adversity (maltreatment or neglect) assessed via the Childhood Trauma Questionnaire (CTQ) and validated with the Early Trauma Inventory (ETI). The study also measured cortisol stress reactivity using the Trier Social Stress Test (TSST).
* **Methodological Summary:** Linear regression models were used to test associations, controlling for age, sex, and cell composition. Statistical significance for the targeted replication of the KITLG locus was set at a gene-wide corrected threshold of p < 0.00263. For exploratory genome-wide analyses, False Discovery Rate (FDR) correction was applied.

## Summary Notes

* **Non-Replication of KITLG:** The study failed to replicate previous findings that suggested KITLG DNA methylation mediates the link between childhood trauma and cortisol stress reactivity. The authors suggest this may be due to the use of purified monocytes rather than heterogeneous tissues like whole blood or buccal cells.
* **Regional Findings:** While no individual CpG sites reached genome-wide significance, regional analysis (DMRs) identified two significant areas mapping to non-coding genes (LINC01725/MIR548AP and LOC101929241) associated with cortisol stress reactivity.
* **HPA Axis Dysregulation:** The study confirmed the well-established link between childhood adversity and significantly lower cortisol reactivity to stress (blunted HPA axis response), even though the epigenetic mediation via KITLG was not supported in this tissue type.
* **Methodological Caveats:** Major differences between this study and the original discovery cohort include the older age of participants, higher severity of trauma, and the use of serum instead of saliva for cortisol assessment. The authors conclude that monocytes may not be suitable biomarkers for capturing KITLG methylation differences related to childhood adversity.
