# Childhood adversity and DNA methylation in two population-based cohorts

**Reference:** Houtepen, L. C., Hardy, R., Maddock, J., Kuh, D., Anderson, E. L., Relton, C. L., Suderman, M. J., & Howe, L. D. (2018). Childhood adversity and DNA methylation in two population-based cohorts. *Translational Psychiatry*, 8(1), 266. [PMID: 30510187]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study involved two cohorts of mid-life women:
* **ALSPAC:** 780 mothers, mean age 47.1 years (range 44–50).
* **NSHD:** 552 women, mean age 53.4 years.


* **Sample Type:** 
* **ALSPAC:** Peripheral blood.
* **NSHD:** Buccal epithelial cells (primary) and whole blood (subsample of 156).


* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Seven adverse childhood experiences (ACEs): parental physical illness, parental mental illness, parental death, parental separation, suboptimal maternal bonding, childhood illness, and child maltreatment. An aggregate "ACE score" was also analyzed.
* **Methodological Summary:** 
* **Covariates:** Adjusted for age at DNA sampling, technical batch effects (independent surrogate variables), and cellular heterogeneity (in ALSPAC blood samples).
* **Significance Thresholds:** Individual CpG sites were considered significant at a False Discovery Rate (FDR) < 0.05. Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs) were identified using Comb-P with a Šidák-corrected p-value < 0.05.

## Summary Notes

* **Parental Health Impact:** The study suggests that adverse experiences related to parental health (mental illness, physical illness, and death) have a more detectable and persistent impact on the offspring's methylome in mid-life compared to other types of adversity.
* **Regional vs. Site-Specific Changes:** While individual CpG site associations failed to replicate across cohorts, nine Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs) were successfully replicated, indicating that childhood adversity may affect broader genomic regions rather than single isolated sites.
* **Biological Mechanism (TONSL):** The most robustly associated DMR (linked to the cumulative ACE score) is located near the promoter of the *TONSL* gene, which is involved in DNA repair and chromatin organization, suggesting a possible molecular link between early stress and cellular maintenance.
* **Cross-Tissue Consistency:** DNA methylation changes associated with childhood adversity showed moderate to high correlation between buccal and blood tissues, supporting the use of peripheral tissues as valid surrogates for studying these epigenetic imprints.
