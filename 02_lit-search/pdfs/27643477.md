# Epigenetic signatures of childhood abuse and neglect

**Reference:** Cecil, C. A., Smith, R. G., Walton, E., Mill, J., McCrory, E. J., & Viding, E. (2016). Epigenetic signatures of childhood abuse and neglect: Implications for psychiatric vulnerability. *Journal of Psychiatric Research*, 83, 184-194. [PMID: 27643477]

## Study Overview

* **Population:** 124 high-risk youth (16–24 years) from inner-city London.
* **Sample Type:** Buccal epithelial cells.
* **Platform:** Illumina HumanMethylation450 BeadChip.
* **Exposure:** 5 maltreatment types (Physical, Sexual, Emotional Abuse; Physical, Emotional Neglect) via Childhood Trauma Questionnaire (CTQ).

## Summary Notes

* Emotional abuse and emotional neglect did not show significant associations after FDR correction.
* The study highlights a molecular link between maltreatment and poor health outcomes, particularly neurodevelopmental and stress-related pathways.
* The shared signature was enriched for biological processes related to neural development and organismal growth.
