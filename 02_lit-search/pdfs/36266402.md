# Epigenetic impact of a 1-week intensive multimodal group program for adolescents with multiple adverse childhood experiences

**Reference:** Kaliman, P., Cosín-Tomás, M., Madrid, A., López, S. R., Llanez-Anaya, E., Papale, L. A., Alisch, R. S., & Davidson, R. J. (2022). Epigenetic impact of a 1-week intensive multimodal group program for adolescents with multiple adverse childhood experiences. *Scientific Reports*, 12(1), 17177. [PMID: 36266402]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** 44 adolescent girls (n=44), aged 13–16 years. Participants were recruited from the foster care system in Colombia and had experienced 4 or more Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs).
* **Sample Type:** Saliva
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina HumanMethylationEPIC Beadchip Array (>850,000 sites)
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Impact of a 1-week intensive multimodal group intervention (mindfulness, artistic expression, and EMDR group therapy) compared to a control group.
* **Methodological Summary:** Changes were assessed from baseline (T1) to post-intervention (T2). Covariates controlled for included baseline DNA methylation levels, BMI, age, ACEs score, and cell type proportions. Statistical significance for intervention-sensitive loci was defined as a p-value < 0.001 and a DNA methylation change > 10%.

## Summary Notes

* **Pathway Enrichment:** The intervention-sensitive loci were significantly enriched in biological processes linked to neural signaling (glutamatergic, serotoninergic, and dopaminergic synapses), immune responses (inflammation regulation via IL8 and CXCR1/2), and endocrine pathways (thyroid hormone and androgen receptor signaling).
* **Disease Relevance:** Functional interactions were found for processes involved in cancer, cardiovascular health, and diabetes—conditions frequently associated with high ACE scores in adulthood.
* **Epigenetic Aging:** While a correlation was found between history of abuse and accelerated epigenetic aging (IEAA) at baseline, the 1-week intervention did not significantly change the epigenetic age trajectory, likely due to the short duration of the program.
* **Trauma Modulation:** The study identified two specific genes, SIRT5 and TRAPPC2L, where methylation changes directly correlated with improvements in clinical PTSD symptoms (CPSS and SPRINT scales), suggesting these may be molecular targets for therapeutic recovery from childhood trauma.
