# Exposure to childhood abuse is associated with human sperm DNA methylation

**Reference:** Roberts, A. L., Gladish, N., Gatev, E., Jones, M. J., Chen, Y., Macisaac, J. L., Tworoger, S. S., Austin, S. B., Tanrikut, C., Chavarro, J. E., Baccarelli, A. A., & Kobor, M. S. (2018). Exposure to childhood abuse is associated with human sperm DNA methylation. *Translational Psychiatry*, 8, 194. [PMID: 30279435]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study involved 34 men from the Growing Up Today Study (GUTS), a longitudinal cohort. The participants had a mean age of 25.7 years (range 23–29). The sample included 17 men exposed to high abuse, 5 to medium abuse, and 12 to no abuse. The cohort was predominantly white (91.2%).
* **Sample Type:** Human sperm cells, purified using a differential lysis method to remove epithelial and round cells.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 (450K) BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Experiences of physical, emotional, and sexual abuse before age 18, measured using the Childhood Trauma Questionnaire (CTQ) and the Conflict Tactics Scale (CTS).
* **Methodological Summary:**
* **Covariates:** Age and semen volume were used in base models. Exploratory models included smoking, BMI, depressive symptoms, posttraumatic stress symptoms, and adulthood trauma exposure.
* **Statistical Thresholds:** For Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs), the criteria were a False Discovery Rate (FDR) <= 0.05, a mean methylation difference (delta beta) >= 5%, and a requirement of at least 3 probes per region.

## Summary Notes

* **Functional Pathways:** The differentially methylated regions were located on genes associated with a variety of critical functions, including neuronal function and neurodegeneration (MAPT, CLU), fat cell regulation (PRDM16), and immune system function (SDK1). These findings align with known systemic effects of childhood abuse on the brain and physical health.
* **Concordance of Methods:** The study demonstrated high internal consistency, as findings from Principal Component Analysis (PCA), DMR analysis, and Machine Learning approaches significantly overlapped. Specifically, 35 of the 63 CpG sites identified in DMRs were also top loaders for the principal component (PC4) associated with abuse.
* **Mediation Factors:** Adulthood mental health (depressive/PTSD symptoms) and lifetime trauma exposure were found to partly mediate the association between childhood abuse and sperm DNA methylation in several DMRs, whereas health behaviors like BMI and smoking did not.
* **Study Limitations:** The authors noted significant caveats, including a small sample size (n=34) and a predominantly white population, which limits the generalizability of the results and necessitates larger replication studies to confirm specific genomic targets.
