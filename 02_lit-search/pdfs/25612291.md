# Borderline personality disorder and childhood maltreatment: a genome-wide methylation analysis

**Reference:** Prados, J., Stenz, L., Courtet, P., Prada, P., Nicastro, R., Adouan, W., Guillaume, S., Olié, E., Aubry, J. M., Ayer, A., & Perroud, N. (2015). Borderline personality disorder and childhood maltreatment: a genome-wide methylation analysis. *Genes, Brain and Behavior*, 14(2), 177-188. [PMID: 25612291]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study included 189 total subjects: 96 patients with Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) and 93 control patients with Major Depressive Disorder (MDD). The BPD group was 91.67% female with a mean age of 32.17, while the MDD group was 63.44% female with a mean age of 41.39.
* **Sample Type:** DNA was extracted from peripheral blood leukocytes (white blood cells).
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium Human Methylation450 BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) status and severity of childhood maltreatment as measured by the Childhood Trauma Questionnaire (CTQ), including sexual, physical, and emotional abuse/neglect.
* **Methodological Summary:** Data were normalized using the 'methylumi' R-package. To account for cellular heterogeneity, cell type proportions (CD8T, CD4T, NK, B-cell, Monocytes, and Granulocytes) were estimated and tested as potential confounders. Statistical analyses included multivariate linear Support Vector Machine (SVM) models with L1-regularization for prediction, and univariate t-tests or Spearman correlation tests. Significance for univariate tests required a methylation difference higher than 10%. Age and gender were addressed as confounding variables.

## Summary Notes

* **Key Finding (miR124-3):** The most significant and consistent finding across multiple analyses was the differential methylation of cg04927004, located near **miR124-3**. BPD subjects and those with more severe childhood maltreatment exhibited **lower methylation levels** at this site. miR124-3 is highly expressed in neurons and regulates genes involved in the HPA axis (e.g., **NR3C1**, **NR3C2**) and neural plasticity.
* **Enriched Biological Pathways:** Functional enrichment analysis identified several key biological clusters associated with the identified genes, including **SH3 domains** (involved in signal transduction), **apoptosis/programmed cell death**, **GTPase regulator activity**, and **cation/ion binding** (specifically potassium channels like **KCNQ2**).
* **Methodological Considerations:** The authors utilized MDD patients as a control group to specifically isolate BPD-related epigenetic signatures from those related to depression or suicide attempts. They also implemented a rigorous bioinformatics approach to ensure that observed methylation differences were not simply artifacts of varying blood cell compositions.
* **Study Caveats:** Limitations include the use of self-reported retrospective data for childhood trauma and the fact that peripheral blood methylation may not perfectly reflect epigenetic status in the brain. Additionally, while the miR124-3 finding was validated via pyrosequencing, the correlation between platforms was modest (r = 0.27).
