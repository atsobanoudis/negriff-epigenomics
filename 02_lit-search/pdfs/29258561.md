# A pilot investigation on DNA methylation modifications associated with complex posttraumatic symptoms in elderly traumatized in childhood

**Reference:** Marinova, Z., Maercker, A., Grünblatt, E., Wojdacz, T. K., & Walitza, S. (2017). A pilot investigation on DNA methylation modifications associated with complex posttraumatic symptoms in elderly traumatized in childhood. *BMC Research Notes*, 10, 752. [PMID: 29258561]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study included 31 elderly individuals (17 males and 14 females) who were former Swiss indentured child laborers (Verdingkinder). The mean age was 76.4 years (± 6.3). The mean age at the start of indenture was 4.7 years (± 4.5), and the mean duration of indenture was 11.8 years (± 5).
* **Sample Type:** DNA was extracted from buccal epithelial cells (buccal swabs).
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 BeadChip (450k chip), encompassing approximately 485,000 methylation sites.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Complex posttraumatic stress disorder (CPTSD) symptoms and symptom complexity, evaluated using the Trauma Symptom Inventory (TSI). Specific domains included dissociation, tension reduction behavior, and dysfunctional sexual behavior.
* **Methodological Summary:** * **Covariates:** Age, gender, estimated proportion of buccal epithelial cells, array, and slide.
* **Statistical Threshold:** The threshold for experiment-wide statistical significance was set at 5 x 10^-7 to account for multiple testing.
* **Software:** Analysis was performed in R using the *minfi* package for preprocessing and *CpGassoc* for correlation analysis.

## Summary Notes

* **Enriched Biological Pathways:** The identified genes are primarily involved in neuronal function and posttranslational modifications. Specifically, HAP1 is linked to neuronal survival and neurogenesis; RANBP2 is involved in SUMOylation; PSMA4 is a subunit of the proteasome system regulating memory formation; and CARNS1 is involved in the synthesis of antioxidants (carnosine).
* **Clinical Relevance:** The study found stronger associations between DNA methylation profiles and "complex" posttraumatic symptoms (ICD-11 CPTSD) compared to "classical" PTSD symptoms (e.g., anxious arousal, intrusive experiences), suggesting an epigenetic basis for symptom complexity.
* **Study Caveats:** The authors noted several limitations, including the small pilot sample size (n=31), the cross-sectional design which prevents causal inference, and the use of peripheral buccal tissue as a surrogate for brain-based epigenetic changes.
* **Functional Implications:** The correlation between CARNS1 methylation and symptom complexity suggests that pathways related to oxidative stress response may be epigenetically modulated in individuals with prolonged childhood trauma.
