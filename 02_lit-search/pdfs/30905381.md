# Sensitive periods for the effect of childhood adversity on DNA methylation: Results from a prospective, longitudinal study

**Reference:** Dunn, E. C., Soare, T. W., Zhu, Y., Simpkin, A. J., Suderman, M. J., Klengel, T., Smith, A. D., Ressler, K., & Relton, C. L. (2019). Sensitive periods for the effect of childhood adversity on DNA methylation: Results from a prospective, longitudinal study. *Biological Psychiatry*, 85(10), 838-849. [PMID: 30905381]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study used data from the Accessible Resource for Integrated Epigenomics Studies (ARIES), a subsample of the Avon Longitudinal Study of Parents and Children (ALSPAC). The analytic sample size ranged from **n=691 to 774** mother-child pairs.
* **Sample Type:** DNA was extracted from **peripheral blood leukocytes** (collected at age 7) and **cord blood** (used for sensitivity analyses).
* **EWAS Platform:** **Illumina Infinium Human Methylation 450k BeadChip** microarray.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Seven types of childhood adversity measured repeatedly across three developmental periods (Very Early Childhood: <3 years; Early Childhood: 3-5 years; Middle Childhood: 6-7 years):
1. Caregiver physical or emotional abuse
2. Sexual or physical abuse (by anyone)
3. Maternal psychopathology
4. One adult in the household
5. Family instability
6. Financial stress/poverty
7. Neighborhood disadvantage/poverty


* **Methodological Summary:** The study utilized a two-stage **Structured Life Course Modeling Approach (SLCMA)** using **Least Angle Regression (LARS)** to compare three theoretical models: Sensitive Periods, Accumulation, and Recency.
* **Covariates:** Child race/ethnicity, birth weight, maternal age, number of previous pregnancies, sustained maternal smoking during pregnancy, parent social class, and cell type heterogeneity.
* **Significance Thresholds:** Epigenome-wide significance was set at **p < 1 x 10^-7** (Bonferroni) and **FDR q < 0.05**.

## Summary Notes

* **Dominance of Sensitive Periods:** The timing of adversity was a far more significant predictor of DNA methylation than the total accumulation or the recency of events. Specifically, the first three years of life (very early childhood) emerged as a critical sensitive period, accounting for the majority of the differentially methylated loci.
* **Biological Pathway Enrichment:** Genes near the 380 FDR-significant sites were enriched for Gene Ontology (GO) terms related to development and neurological function, including **positive regulation of developmental growth**, **axon development**, and **neuron apoptotic process**.
* **Gene Constraint:** Genes associated with adversity-induced methylation changes showed higher evidence of functional constraint (measured by probability of intolerance to loss-of-function variation), suggesting these genes play essential roles in survival and reproduction.
* **Methodological Caveats:** The researchers noted that while peripheral blood DNAm provides a valuable biomarker, it may not perfectly reflect changes in the brain. However, they found modest positive correlations between blood and brain tissue for the identified top CpG sites. Additionally, they highlighted that traditional "ever vs. never" exposure classifications might dilute findings by ignoring developmental timing.
