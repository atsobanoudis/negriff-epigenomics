# A genome-wide methylation study reveals X chromosome and childhood trauma methylation alterations associated with borderline personality disorder

**Reference:** Arranz, M. J., Gallego-Fabrega, C., Martín-Blanco, A., Soler, J., Elices, M., Dominguez-Clavé, E., Salazar, J., Vegas, D., Briones-Buixassa, L., & Pascual, J. C. (2021). A genome-wide methylation study reveals X chromosome and childhood trauma methylation alterations associated with borderline personality disorder. *Translational Psychiatry*, 11(1), 5. [PMID: 33414392]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:**
* **Discovery Sample:** 140 female subjects of Caucasian origin (44 healthy controls, 47 BPD patients without childhood trauma [BPD-T], and 49 BPD patients with childhood trauma [BPD+T]). Age range: 18–50 years.
* **Replication Sample:** 407 female subjects of Caucasian origin (114 healthy controls and 293 BPD patients). Within the BPD group, 147 had trauma information (81 BPD+T and 66 BPD-T).


* **Sample Type:** DNA extracted from whole blood.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450K BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) diagnosis and history of childhood trauma (sexual, physical, or emotional abuse; physical or emotional neglect) assessed via the Childhood Trauma Questionnaire-Short Form (CTQ-SF).
* **Methodological Summary:**
* **Covariates:** Analyses were adjusted for age, principal components (PC), and in specific comparisons, the presence/absence of childhood trauma.
* **Statistical Thresholds:** For the genome-wide study, significance was set at p-value < 10^-07. False Discovery Rate (FDR) adjusted p-values (q-values) were also reported. Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs) used a threshold of p-value < 0.05.

## Summary Notes

* **Gender-Specific Mechanisms:** The study identified significant hypomethylation in five X chromosome regions (including *PQBP1*, *ZNF41*, and *RPL10*) in BPD patients. The authors conclude these epigenetic alterations, along with enriched estrogen-regulation pathways, may explain the higher prevalence and differential presentation of BPD in females.
* **Impact of Childhood Trauma:** Childhood trauma was found to modulate the magnitude of epigenetic alterations. Specifically, BPD patients with trauma (BPD+T) showed more pronounced methylation differences compared to controls than those without trauma. Trauma-associated genes were distinct from general BPD genes and were located on autosomal chromosomes (e.g., *POU5F1*, *GPR55*, *PXDN*).
* **Biological Pathways:** Gene set enrichment analyses (GSEA) revealed that differentially methylated genes are primarily involved in neurogenesis, neuron/cell differentiation, estrogen response, and immune response (specifically antigen processing in the MHC locus of chromosome 6).
* **Study Limitations:** The authors noted limitations including a moderate discovery sample size, lack of medication and smoking history data, and the use of peripheral blood as a proxy for brain tissue (though they cite a high correlation of 0.86 between blood and brain methylation).
