# DNA methylation profiles of elderly individuals subjected to indentured childhood labor and trauma

**Reference:** Marinova, Z., Maercker, A., Küffer, A., Robinson, M. D., Wojdacz, T. K., Walitza, S., Grünblatt, E., & Burri, A. (2017). DNA methylation profiles of elderly individuals subjected to indentured childhood labor and trauma. *BMC Medical Genetics*, 18(21). [PMID: 28241754]

---

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study included 45 elderly Swiss individuals. The "case" group consisted of 30 former indentured child laborers ("Verdingkinder") with a mean age of 75.9 years. The control group consisted of 15 individuals with similar demographic characteristics and a mean age of 72.8 years.
* **Sample Type:** DNA was isolated from epithelial buccal cells (sampled using buccal swabs).
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium 450 k DNA methylation array (covering 485,000 methylation sites).
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Severe childhood adversity and trauma resulting from indentured childhood labor. Trauma was quantified using the Childhood Trauma Questionnaire short form (CTQ-SF).
* **Methodological Summary:**
* **Covariates:** Primary analysis adjusted for age, gender, batch effects, and multiple testing. Secondary analyses additionally adjusted for major depressive disorder (MDD), PTSD screen results, coronary heart disease, diabetes, length of education, and estimated proportion of buccal epithelial cells.
* **Significance Threshold:** False Discovery Rate (FDR) calculated by the Benjamini-Hochberg method set at < 0.05.

## Summary Notes

* **Biological Pathway Enrichment:** Gene enrichment analysis revealed that differentially methylated genes are primarily involved in **neuronal development**, **axon guidance**, **neuronal projection/dendrites**, and **cellular component organization**. Key genes include *MTOR*, *PLXNB1*, *ROBO1*, *DLGAP2*, and *CACNA1H*.
* **Adaptor Protein Involvement:** The gene showing the strongest differential methylation was *SKAP2* (Src kinase-associated phosphoprotein 2), which had five hypermethylated CpG sites in its gene body. *SKAP2* is an adaptor protein involved in src signaling, cell migration, and potentially neurodegenerative processes.
* **Persistence into Old Age:** The study provides evidence that specific epigenetic alterations associated with childhood trauma can persist or be detectable in individuals in their mid-70s, despite the natural "epigenetic aging" process.
* **Study Limitations:** The authors noted limitations including a relatively small sample size (N=45), a cross-sectional design, and potential confounding from the high prevalence of comorbidities and medication use in an elderly population. They also noted that using buccal cells as a peripheral tissue proxy for brain-based processes is a limitation, though systemic epigenetic responses to trauma are possible.
