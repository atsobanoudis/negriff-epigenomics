# Maternal epigenetic index links early neglect to later neglectful care and other psychopathological, cognitive, and bonding effects

**Reference:** León, I., Góngora, D., Rodrigo, M. J., Herrero-Roldán, S., López Rodríguez, M., Mitchell, C., Fisher, J., & Iturria-Medina, Y. (2025). Maternal epigenetic index links early neglect to later neglectful care and other psychopathological, cognitive, and bonding effects. *Clinical Epigenetics*, 17(46). [PMID: 40057810]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study included 138 biological mothers (51 in the neglectful group; 87 in the control group). The mean age of mothers was 31.71 years in the neglectful group and 34.70 years in the control group. Participants were recruited from School Centers, Municipal Social Services, and Primary Health Centers in Tenerife, Spain.
* **Sample Type:** Saliva (DNA extracted from saliva samples).
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Human Methylation EPIC BeadChip v1.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Childhood maltreatment (specifically physical neglect) and current neglectful parenting behavior.
* **Methodological Summary:** The study utilized a machine learning algorithm for Trajectory Inference (TI) to derive a compound Epigenetic Maternal Neglect (EMN) score. Covariates controlled for included education level, mother's age, plate position (experimental variables), and cell type proportions (leukocyte and epithelial cell concentrations). The significance threshold for initial CpG selection was p <= 0.02138 (the top 1% of p-values from group comparisons). Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) was used to test the link between past neglect and current outcomes.

## Summary Notes

* **Biological Pathways:** Gene enrichment analysis revealed that genes influenced by maternal neglect are heavily involved in cognitive impairment, neurodegenerative disorders (such as Alzheimer's and senile dementia), and psychopathological conditions (including schizophrenia, autism, depression, and anxiety).
* **Intergenerational Link:** The study provides a biological framework for the behavioral transmission of neglect, suggesting that a mother's own childhood physical neglect creates an "epigenetic load" (EMN score) that predicts her subsequent neglectful caregiving and poor emotional availability with her own child.
* **Epigenetic Age Acceleration:** A significant correlation (r = 0.24, p = 0.0044) was found between the EMN score and the PhenoAge clock, indicating that the neglectful condition is associated with accelerated biological aging.
* **Study Caveats:** Key limitations include a relatively small sample size (n=138), the cross-sectional design which prevents establishing definitive causality, and the lack of SNP genotyping to account for the impact of genetic variation on methylation data.
