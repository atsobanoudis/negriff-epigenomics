# Epigenetics of early-life adversity in youth: cross-sectional and longitudinal associations

**Reference:** Sumner, J. A., Gambazza, S., Gao, X., Baccarelli, A. A., Uddin, M., & McLaughlin, K. A. (2022). Epigenetics of early-life adversity in youth: cross-sectional and longitudinal associations. *Clinical Epigenetics*, 14(1), 48. [PMID: 35395780]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The analytic sample consisted of 113 children and adolescents (42.5% female) recruited from the community. The mean age at baseline was 12.2 years (range 8.0–17.0 years). The sample was diverse with respect to race/ethnicity (44.2% White, 21.2% Black, 11.5% Latino, 10.6% Asian, 12.4% Other).
* **Sample Type:** DNA derived from saliva samples collected at baseline and an approximately 2-year follow-up.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina EPIC BeadChip array (measuring >850,000 CpG sites).
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Early-life adversity (ELA) dimensions of **threat** (assessed via physical, sexual, and emotional abuse) and **deprivation** (assessed via emotional and physical neglect). Both dichotomous (presence/absence) and continuous (frequency/severity) measures were used.
* **Methodological Summary:**
* **Statistical Models:** Linear mixed effects models (cross-sectional) and generalized linear models (longitudinal).
* **Covariates:** Age, sex, family income-to-needs ratio, cell-type principal components (PCs), ancestry PCs (population stratification), and random batch effects. Sensitivity analyses also adjusted for tobacco use.
* **Significance Threshold:** False Discovery Rate (FDR) controlled at < 0.05 for epigenome-wide analyses.

## Summary Notes

* **Dimensional Specificity:** The study provides evidence that threat (abuse) and deprivation (neglect) are associated with distinct epigenetic signatures. For instance, in longitudinal analyses, neglect over follow-up was associated with change in DNAm at a site in the *ZFAT* gene, whereas abuse was not.
* **Biological Pathways:** Significant genes associated with abuse (e.g., *SH3PXD2A*, *CORIN*, *VASN*) are involved in cellular migration, vascular repair, and energy homeostasis, suggesting potential mechanisms for increased risk of cancer, cardiovascular disease, and obesity.
* **Change Over Time:** This study is one of the first to show longitudinal change in DNAm in relation to ongoing adversity during youth, though the authors note that overall DNAm patterns showed substantial stability over the 2-year period.
* **Study Caveats:** Key limitations include a small sample size (n=113), the use of saliva (which may not reflect brain-specific processes), and the lack of genetic data to control for methylation quantitative trait loci (mQTLs).
