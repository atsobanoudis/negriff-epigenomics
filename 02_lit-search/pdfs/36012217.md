# Experiences of Trauma and DNA Methylation Profiles among African American Mothers and Children

**Reference:** Barcelona, V., Huang, Y., Caceres, B. A., Newhall, K. P., Hui, Q., Cerdeña, J. P., Crusto, C. A., Sun, Y. V., & Taylor, J. Y. (2022). Experiences of Trauma and DNA Methylation Profiles among African American Mothers and Children. *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*, 23(16), 8951. [PMID: 36012217]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study included a total of 500 participants consisting of 236 African American mothers (mean age 31.2 years) and 232 children (mean age 3.7 years).
* **Sample Type:** DNA was extracted from saliva samples using Oragene-500 format tubes.
* **EWAS Platform:** The Illumina Infinium Methylation EPIC (850K) BeadChip was used to quantify DNA methylation.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** 
    * **Mothers:** Lifetime potentially traumatic events measured by the Life Events Checklist-5 (LEC).
    * **Children:** Childhood trauma exposure measured by the Traumatic Events Screening Inventory-Parent Report Revised (TESI-PRR) and the LEC.


* **Methodological Summary:** 
    * **Covariates:** Adjusted for age (maternal and child), maternal smoking, child sex, batch effects (chip/batch), population stratification (top 10 principal components), and cell-type proportions (estimated via the reference-free "RefFreeEWAS" method).
    * **Significance Threshold:** Statistical significance was defined as a False Discovery Rate (FDR) corrected q-value < 0.05.

## Summary Notes

* **Biological Relevance:** The identified ENOX1 gene is associated with mental health outcomes, including schizophrenia and depression. The study suggests that DNA methylation changes at this site may serve as a biological marker for early-life trauma in African American children.
* **Tissue Specificity:** The researchers utilized saliva because it is a non-invasive medium suitable for young children. While saliva is more heterogeneous than blood, the authors noted that it correlates well with blood and may be particularly relevant for studies involving mental health outcomes.
* **Study Strengths & Limitations:** A major strength is the focus on an understudied, high-risk population (African American mother-child dyads). A primary limitation noted was the potential for social desirability bias, where mothers might underreport traumatic events, which would likely bias results toward the null.
* **Functional Implications:** The findings demonstrate that epigenetic modifications can be detected in very young children (ages 3–5) following traumatic exposures, highlighting the potential for early-life stress to shape physiological development via molecular pathways.
