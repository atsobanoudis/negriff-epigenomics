# Violent aggression predicted by multiple pre-adult environmental hits

**Reference:** Mitjans, M., Seidel, J., Begemann, M., Bockhop, F., Moya-Higueras, J., Bansal, V., ... & Ehrenreich, H. (2019). Violent aggression predicted by multiple pre-adult environmental hits. *Molecular Psychiatry*, 24(10), 1549-1564. [PMID: 29795411]

## Study Overview

* **Population & Demographics:** The study utilized 6 independent cohorts. This included 4 schizophrenia samples (Discovery sample n=134; Replication I n=606; Replication II n=320; Replication III n=503) and 2 Spanish general population samples (Replication IV n=336; Replication V n=229). Participants were predominantly young adults; for example, the discovery sample had a mean age of approximately 33 years.
* **Sample Type:** Peripheral whole blood-derived DNA was used for the methylation array, and peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMC) were used for mRNA expression analysis.
* **EWAS Platform:** Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450K BeadChip.
* **Primary Exposure(s):** Accumulation of pre-adult environmental "hits" (before age 18), specifically: urbanicity, migration, physical abuse, sexual abuse, cannabis consumption, and alcohol abuse.
* **Methodological Summary:** Statistical analysis included linear regression models for methylation data. Covariates controlled for included age, medication, and estimated cell proportions (monocytes, granulocytes, CD4T, CD8T, natural killer, and B-cells). The primary significance threshold for the EWAS was set at p < 10^-8, with exploratory lowering to 10^-6.

## Summary Notes

* **Epigenetic Umbrella Mediator:** While individual CpG site analysis was largely negative, the authors identified **HDAC1** as an "umbrella mediator" of epigenetic processes. They found significantly increased HDAC1 mRNA levels in the high-risk group, suggesting it serves as a more robust readout of lasting epigenetic alterations in smaller sample sizes than specific methylation sites.
* **Equifinality of Risks:** The study concludes that different environmental hits (e.g., abuse, migration, urbanicity) are to some degree interchangeable and cumulative. No single factor was a dominant predictor; rather, the **accumulation** of three or more hits strongly predicted violent aggression (Odds Ratio 10.5).
* **Disease Independence:** A major conclusion is that the relationship between pre-adult risk accumulation and violent aggression is **independent of mental illness**, as the "stair pattern" of increasing aggression with increasing risk was observed in both schizophrenia and general population cohorts.
* **Methodological Caveats:** The authors noted that the lack of significant findings in the epigenome-wide scan (EWAS) might be due to the vast heterogeneity of individual methylation sites, pathogenetic complexity, and a sample size that, while standard, may have lacked the power to overcome these factors.
